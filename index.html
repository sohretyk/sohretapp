How It Works

Your complete market research workflow

1

Enter Your Keyword or Niche

Start by entering any product keyword, niche, or market segment you're interested in exploring. For example: "vintage cat art", "minimalist wall decor", or "personalized gifts".

2

Analyze Market Data

Our platform analyzes publicly available Etsy marketplace data to understand the competitive landscape, search demand, and market trends for your keyword. This is read-only analysis of public information.

3

View Competition Metrics

See clear competition indicators: Is this niche saturated (High), balanced (Medium), or underserved (Low)? Understand how many sellers are active and what the market density looks like.

4

Discover Opportunities

Our opportunity scoring algorithm identifies keywords and niches where demand exists but competition is manageable. Find the sweet spot for your business.

5

Explore Related Keywords

Discover related keywords and variations that might offer better opportunities. Expand your research with similar terms, long-tail keywords, and niche variations.

6

Analyze Successful Patterns

Review aggregated insights about successful listings in your niche. Understand patterns in pricing, product types, and market positioning - without copying specific designs.

7

Track Trends Over Time

Monitor how your chosen niches evolve. Is competition increasing? Are new trends emerging? Stay informed with ongoing market intelligence.

8

Save Your Research

Bookmark promising keywords and niches for future reference. Build your research library and compare opportunities side-by-side.

9

Make Informed Decisions

Use all the insights gathered to make strategic business decisions. What products should you create? Which niches should you enter? Our data helps you decide with confidence.

We store the keywords you search, saved research items, and bookmarked opportunities within our platform.

We access publicly available Etsy marketplace data through Etsy's API for research and analysis purposes. This data is:

  • Cached temporarily for performance (no long-term storage)
